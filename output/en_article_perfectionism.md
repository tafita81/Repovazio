# Perfectionism Is Not About Standards. Science Shows It Is About Fear.

*By Daniela Coelho · Behavioral Research & Psychology*

---

Perfectionism is one of the most common answers in job interviews — as if it were a charming flaw.

But clinical perfectionism — the kind that paralyzes, never finishes, never feels good enough — is not charming. It is a form of anxiety with a high cost.

## What Hewitt and Flett Found

Paul Hewitt and Gordon Flett identified three types. Self-oriented: impossibly high standards for yourself. Socially prescribed: the belief that others require perfection from you. Other-oriented: requiring perfection from others.

The most psychologically damaging is socially prescribed perfectionism — because the standard is external and therefore never secure.

## Brene Brown's Definition

Brown at the University of Texas is direct: perfectionism is not about doing your best. It is about trying to earn approval and acceptance. It is a shield — a way to prevent judgment by achieving some standard that would make criticism impossible.

The problem: the standard is unreachable by design. That is what makes it effective as an anxiety mechanism.

## Five Differences From Healthy Excellence

Excellence allows a good enough version. Perfectionism never delivers while imperfect — which is always. Excellence learns from mistakes. Perfectionism is devastated by them. Excellence has pleasure in process. Perfectionism is obsessed with external judgment. Excellence finishes. Perfectionism procrastinates because finishing means being evaluated.

Done is better than perfect is not laziness. It is health.
